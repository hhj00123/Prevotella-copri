
# Prevotella-copri
Prevotella copri has been implicated in human health and a wide range of diseases including inflammatory disorders such as rheumatoid arthritis (RA), yet the underlying mechanisms remain incompletely understood, partly due to the strain diversity of P. copri and its resistance to be cultivated. In this study, we developed a selective medium with xylan as the sole carbon source (mX medium) and established an efficient method for the targeted isolation of P. copri strains. Using this approach, we obtained 34 strains of P. copri from RA patients and healthy donors. Genomic analysis revealed substantial strain-level diversity of P. copri, accompanied by a diverse repertoire of rearrangement hotspot (Rhs) toxin genes. We further identified and functionally evaluated a genetic cluster encoding for a novel TC-like toxin complex. The gene of the TC-like toxin was disrupted. The resulting mutant and the wildtype were gavaged into model mice. Results showed that this TC-like toxin disrupted E-cadherin-mediated epithelial integrity in mice, resulting in LPS translocation across the epithelial barrier, and subsequently activating the TLR4-mediated inflammatory pathway, thereby promoting systemic inflammation and exacerbating arthritis. In summary, these findings reveal that P. copri has diverse strains and a novel TC-like toxin complex. The results provide new mechanistic insight into how P. copri contributes to autoimmune inflammation.
